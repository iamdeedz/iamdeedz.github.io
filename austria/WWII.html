Austria's involvement in WWII

Austria was annexed by Nazi Germany on the 12th of March, 1938 in an event known as the Anschluss and thus became a state of Germany until the end of the war. About 700,000 Austrians or 10% of the population joined the German army during the war.

After the war, Austria was occupied by the Allies and split into four territories: American, British, French and Soviet. On the 15th of May, 1955, representatives from the Americans, British, French and Soviets signed the Austrian State Treaty, declaring an independent Austria and arranging the withdrawal of the Allied occupants.
